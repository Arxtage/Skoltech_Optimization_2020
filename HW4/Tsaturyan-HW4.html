2.¶

To use the theorem [source: https://ljk.imag.fr/membres/Anatoli.Iouditski/cours/convex/chapitre_4.pdf] let us introduce new veariable: $$t=-P^{-1}(A^T\lambda-q)$$

Then the dual problem is rewrited as: $$-\frac{1}{2}t^TPt+b^T\lambda \to \max | A^T\lambda+Pt=q, \lambda \geqslant 0$$

In our case: All the requirements of theorem are fulfiled: $P$ is symmetric positive definite, primal objective at x is equal to the dual objective at $(\lambda,t)$ So, both primal and dual problems are solvable and the pair $(x;(\lambda,t))$ of feasible solutions to the problem is comprised of optimal solutions to them.
